## What is Ketamine and Its Legitimate Uses?
Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic originally developed in the 1960s. In medical settings, it's primarily used for:

- **Pain management**: Administered in hospitals for short-term sedation during procedures.
- **Treatment-resistant depression**: Low-dose ketamine infusions are approved in some UK clinics for mental health conditions, under strict NHS or private supervision.
- **Veterinary applications**: Commonly used in animal care.

In 2026, the UK's Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) continues to regulate ketamine tightly, with prescriptions limited to licensed professionals. Off-label recreational use has surged, but this carries significant health and legal risks.

## The Dangers of Buying Ketamine Online in the UK

- **Health risks**: Overdose, bladder damage (known as \ketamine cystitis\ hallucinations, and long-term cognitive issues.
- **Legal consequences**: Under UK law, buying controlled substances can lead to up to 7 years in prison and fines. Customs seizures are common for international shipments.
- **Scams and exploitation**: Many sites are fraudulent, stealing personal data or delivering nothing.

In 2026, law enforcement agencies like the National Crime Agency actively monitor and shut down illicit online markets.

### Why Avoid Unregulated Sources?
- **Purity issues**: Street or online ketamine may be laced with fentanyl or other substances, increasing overdose risks.
- **Addiction potential**: Regular use can lead to dependency, with UK helplines like FRANK (0300 123 6600) reporting rising cases.
- **Ethical concerns**: Illicit trade funds organized crime, impacting communities.
